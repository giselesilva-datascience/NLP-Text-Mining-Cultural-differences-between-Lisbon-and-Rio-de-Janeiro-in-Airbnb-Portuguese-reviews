# NLP-text-mining-Cultural-differences-between-Lisbon-and-Rio-de-Janeiro-in-Airbnb-Portuguese-reviews
Master's Thesis in Data Science, at Universidade Europeia, Lisbon Portugal.
Scientific supervision by Professor Mariana Sofia Barreira Cavique Santos, Assistant Professor at the European University.

Abstract
Given the growing demand for short-term rental platforms, it is relevant to conduct an exploratory analysis —in Portuguese language— of cultural differences and guest satisfaction. This study proposes a text-mining approach applied to online reviews from Airbnb, comparing the cities of Lisbon and Rio de Janeiro; despite sharing the same language and representing a context that remains under-explored in the literature, these cities exhibit distinct cultural characteristics. The research focuses on 200,000 reviews published between 2010 and 2025. To this end, it employs topic modeling to identify the main themes addressed in the reviews and sentiment analysis to understand levels of guest satisfaction and dissatisfaction.

Data collection and exploration
Inside Airbnb periodically releases new versions of datasets covering various cities worldwide, including information on accommodations, availability calendars for the subsequent 365 days, and reviews for each property. This study utilized two distinct datasets—one for the city of Lisbon (snapshot from September 21, 2025) and one for the city of Rio de Janeiro (snapshot from September 26, 2025)—both obtained from the Inside Airbnb website (https://insideairbnb.com/).

Metodology
This study adopts a methodological approach based on Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, transferring structured knowledge and relevant insights from a dataset of online reviews from the Airbnb platform. The methodological procedure combined the application of the LDA model, rigorous text preprocessing, the construction of a "bag-of-words" corpus, and the evaluation of thematic coherence to select the most suitable model (Blei et al., 2002; Manning et al., 2008; Röder et al., 2015). This sequence allows not only for the identification of latent topics within the reviews but also ensures higher lexical quality and greater consistency in interpreting the results (Newman et al., 2010). Thus, it was possible to extract patterns from large volumes of unstructured text, enabling the identification of frequent characteristics, key terms, and linguistic structures present in guest reviews.

The integration of these methodologies—text mining, topic modeling, and sentiment analysis—allows for a multidimensional understanding of the content of guest reviews by combining the discovery of thematic and cultural patterns. This approach provides a solid foundation for interpreting results and generating scientific and practical contributions, making it possible to understand the main arguments discussed by guests, identify sociocultural patterns, and determine the sentiment polarity associated with the topics discussed.

For this study, the BERTimbau Base model (neuralmind/bert-base-portuguese-cased)—featuring 110 million parameters—was used for sentiment analysis. This model was selected because it was specifically pre-trained for the Portuguese language, making it better suited to capturing semantic nuances, particularly those of Brazilian Portuguese. This choice is justified by the fact that NLP in Portuguese faces unique challenges—such as lexical ambiguity, linguistic variation, abbreviations, and discursive phenomena like irony and sarcasm—which are frequently found in informal texts and on social media (Souza et al., 2020). Subsequently, the (stevillis/bertimbau-finetuned-glassdoor-reviews),  model was also applied; this model is based on BERTimbau Base but was fine-tuned to perform sentiment classification on Glassdoor reviews (Stevillis, 2025). Its use aimed to compare the performance of a generic model against a model pre-trained on reviews, even though the latter were not specifically from Airbnb.(https://huggingface.co/stevillis/bertimbau-finetuned-glassdoor-reviews).

The results of this study have practical implications for hosts, platforms, and policymakers by providing empirical evidence regarding the factors that most influence ratings and the accommodation experience, as well as the varying expectations of guests across different urban destinations, while simultaneously reinforcing the potential of data science to support decision-making in the tourism sector.
